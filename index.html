G'day, I'm Tobin

I'm a data scientist with a passion for interesting problems. I'm from Adelaide, Australia but heading to MIT for a PhD in September on a Fulbright Scholarship.

Previously I completed a Masters of Philosophy in Applied Mathematics and Data Science at The University of Adelaide where my research focused on networks of natural language information flow. Between these studies I have run the Bayesian arm of the COVID-19 forecasting for the Australian Federal government.

More broadly, my interests lie at the intersections of complex networks, machine learning, economics or social science. That's a broad set of intersections, but life is full of interesting questions.

When I'm not doing work, I can often be overheard trying to convince undergraduates to get involved in data science and mathematics. I was Valedictorian of the graduating class of Mathematics, Computer Science and Electrical Engineering, and was an active member of student societies throughout my Bachelors. Before that, I grew up in the "Peanut capital of Australia", the agricultural town of Kingaroy, Queensland.

Don't hesitate to reach out if you have something interesting to talk about.

Professional Work

I work casually as a advisory consultant for NLP and open data projects at the AI consulting group Brainframe. I have been involved since Brainframe acquired my open-data analysis start-up in 2019. Recently, I have co-founded a new start-up called ClearerConsent, which aims to get digitalise our informed consent technology in healthcare to support the culturally diverse backgrounds of Australians.
